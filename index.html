About Me

I am an Italian-American-New-York-Mainer-Inter-Milan-Supporter. My family comes from Friuli and Istria, where there are many endangered languages and lots of efforts to preserve and revitalize them. I try to help by making tools that speed up language documentation.

Research

I am a fourth-year Shelby Davis Scholar at New York University pursuing a joint degree in Mathematics and Computer Science. My research focuses on developing computational tools for low-resource and under-resourced languages, with the goal of making language technologies more inclusive and practically useful for linguistic research and documentation.

My work centers on automatic speech recognition (ASR) and related speech technologies, including forced alignment, speaker diarization, segmentation, and part-of-speech tagging in low-resource settings.
