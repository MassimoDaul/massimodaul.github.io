Biography

Massimo Daul was born in Milan, Italy, and has spent much of his life moving between the east coast of the United States and Europe. After attending UWC Adriatic for two years, he moved to New York City, where he has been studying Mathematics and Computer Science at New York University (NYU) for the past three years. His research focuses on low-resource machine learning, particularly in Natural Language Processing (NLP) and speech processing. Massimo is passionate about developing computational tools to help preserve endangered languages, making linguistic resources more accessible. His work spans speaker diarization, forced alignment, and optimization techniques, with a special interest in applying linear algebra and probabilistic methods to solve discrete alignment problems. He has several published papers, with more currently under review.
